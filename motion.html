Motion Design Work Made with Illustrator and After Effects

New Hope Fellowship Downtown

As a media team volunteer at New Hope Fellowship, the English ministry of the parent church Light Presbyterian Church, I had an opportunity to make a few motion graphics to convey the missions of the creative team, to promote overseas missions and more.

To mark a video made for New Hope Fellowship as their own, I animated their already existing logo. This was used either at the beginning or the end of a video.

To let the members of the church know that there will be overseas missions opportunities, I was asked to make an effective animation in order to convey the message well.

New Hope Fellowship wanted their own creative team to understand what the objective is for the creative ministry. I was asked to make the animation that matches the voice-over with clear and impactful visuals.

Automotive UI

Implementations of augmented reality in cars have been proposed and suggested in all good intentions to improve our lives such as smart car windows. But have there been any for emergency vehicles, which is actually so close in touch with human lives?

A group of alumni including myself, and two professors at Humber College decided to put together an interactive demo representing two head-up displays for emeregency vehicles. And we researched how augmented reality could be implemented in emergency situations.

The video below is the head-up display for a paramedic who is next to the driver's seat (in this case, we are assuming that there will always be a paramedic sitting next to the driver). They will be able to receive a video feed from a bystander who is near the scene, and the video would be scanned to analyze the patient and prepare the paramedics as to how to treat the patient.

Once the paramedics have treated the patient, the dahsboard, which is shown in the video below, will guide them to the nearest hospital that will provide relevant care.

My role in this project was to create a seamless animation to reflect how the AR implementation on an ambulance would work. Our work was selected and presented in the 10th Automotive User Interface Conference which took place in Toronto, Ontario.

Weathery

Weathery is a smartwatch interface design done as a personal project.

It is easy to forget what temperature, or what kind of weather it was supposed to be today. Weathery, the weather watch interface will never let you walk in the snow with your new pair of shoes on.

Having numbers can help, but it is not as efficient as the visuals. Weathery shows you the current weather, and the weather in the near future on your watch screen.

But of course, numbers do matter. You never know if it is going to be extremely cold under a sunny sky. On Weathery, you can just tap on the screen to see the temperature and the date.
