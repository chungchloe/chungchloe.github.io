Application of Augmented Reality for Multi-Scale Interactions in Emergency Vehicles

Adjunct Proceedings of the 10th International ACM Conference on Automotive User Interfaces and Interactive Vehicular Applications

Implementations of augmented reality in cars have been proposed and suggested in all good intentions to improve our lives such as smart car windows. But have there been any for emergency vehicles, which is actually so close in touch with human lives?

A group of alumni including myself, and two professors at Humber College decided to put together an interactive demo representing two head-up displays for emeregency vehicles. And we researched how augmented reality could be implemented in emergency situations.

The video below is the head-up display for a paramedic who is next to the driver's seat (in this case, we are assuming that there will always be a paramedic sitting next to the driver). They will be able to receive a video feed from a bystander who is near the scene, and the video would be scanned to analyze the patient and prepare the paramedics as to how to treat the patient.

Once the paramedics have treated the patient, the dahsboard, which is shown in the video below, will guide them to the nearest hospital that will provide relevant care.

My role in this project was to create a seamless animation to reflect how the AR implementation on an ambulance would work. Our work was selected and presented in the 10th Automotive User Interface Conference which took place in Toronto, Ontario.
